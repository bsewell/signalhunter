Why This Is Different

The expensive part of software was never the code.

It was the communication. The meetings about the meetings. The two-week sprint that delivered what someone could have described in a sentence.

01

Zero Translation Layer

In traditional dev, the person with the idea describes it to a PM, who writes a spec, who hands it to a designer, who hands it to an engineer. Each handoff loses fidelity. Here, the person with the idea talks directly to the agent that writes the code. No telephone game.

02

Institutional Memory

Every decision gets logged: what we chose, what we rejected, and why. Every failure gets documented with root cause and prevention. The AI reads this before every session. It never forgets context, never re-proposes something we already tried and abandoned.

03

The Board Is Real

22 JSONL files. 227 sourced entries. Steve Jobs on why the experience is the product. Peter Gibson on the clinical evidence for FODMAPs. BJ Fogg on why every extra tap is a dropout risk. These aren't vibes — they're indexed, tagged, and routed to the right part of the codebase.
